What is Menko?

Menko (めんこ, 面子), also known as Bettan or Patchin, is a Japanese card game played by two or more players. It is also the name of the type of cards used to play this game. Each player uses Menko cards made from thick paper or cardboard, printed on one or both sides with images from anime, manga, sports, and other works. A player's card is placed on the hardwood or concrete floor, and the other player throws down his card, trying to flip the other player's card with a gust of wind or by striking his card against the other card. If the player succeeds, they take both cards. The player who takes all the cards, or the one with the most cards at the game's end, wins the game.

The pictures on these cards reflect the popular culture of their time, and Menko cards from the past reflect important information about their era. In the Edo and early Meiji period, images like ninja and samurai were popular. Before World War II, the most popular images were of the military, like fighter planes and battleships. After the war, characters from anime and manga were popular, as well as baseball players. Collectors of Japanese baseball cards collect baseball menko.

What is this site all about?

Menko collecting in the United States, and even in Japan, has not had the same kind of widespread collecting culture that other similar hobbies have. Sports cards, tobacco cards, Pokemon, Magic: The Gathering, and others have had a long history of collectors who have compiled information on all the cards in their respective genre. This site is an attempt to bring menko collectors together to begin building out a database of cards.

If you're a menko collector, create an account and start submitting information and images about your cards. Not only will you have an online record of your own collection, you'll also be helping to build out the history of menko cards for all current and future collectors.
